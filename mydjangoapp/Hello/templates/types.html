Types Of Acne


What are the different types of acne?

Blackheads: Open bumps on the skin that fill with excess oil and dead skin. ...
Whiteheads: Bumps that remain closed by oil and dead skin.
Papules: Small red or pink bumps that become inflamed.
Pustules: Pimples containing pus.

Let's discuss about the different types of acne

Blemishes, bumps, pimples, breakouts—there are plenty of words we can use to talk about acne. Because there are so many types of acne and several treatment options, knowing the differences can be tough. Let’s learn about the 6 different types of acne , how they form, what they look like, and what you can do to treat them. The first 2 types of acne we’ll discuss—whiteheads and blackheads—are both non-inflammatory. This type of acne is usually easier to treat than inflammatory acne, so over-the-counter (OTC) treatments may work. The last 4 types—papules, pustules, nodules, and cysts—are types of inflammatory acne that can be harder to treat.
